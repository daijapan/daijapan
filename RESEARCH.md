## Core Question

> **What are the mathematical limits of AI-enabled accessibility, and how can those limits be systematically expanded?**

---

## Research Philosophy

The current generation of AI systems is powerful, but much of the engineering around them remains heuristic.

This research program asks whether AI systems can be understood through mathematical structures that are:

- formal,
- testable,
- compositional,
- and useful for real deployment.

The aim is not to replace engineering judgment, but to give it a mathematical backbone.

---

## Three Research Programs

### 1. Mathematical Accessibility

Accessibility is modeled as a capability space: a set of actions, tools, interfaces, and cognitive workflows that become reachable for a human user through AI.

The key problem is to describe the boundary of that space.

### 2. Reliable Mathematical AI

Reliable AI systems require mathematical constraints, not only prompts and evaluations.

This program studies formal verification, deployment boundaries, workflow policies, and cryptographic interpretability.

### 3. Geometric and Combinatorial Foundations

Pure mathematics provides reusable structures for understanding complex AI and security systems.

This program focuses on toric geometry, relation lattices, rational fans, and local-to-global combinatorial methods.

## Ten-Year Direction

The long-term goal is to build a mathematical theory of AI-enabled human capability.

Such a theory should predict:

- which human limitations can be reduced by AI,
- which limitations remain fundamental,
- what interface or system design changes the boundary,
- and how to measure these changes experimentally.
